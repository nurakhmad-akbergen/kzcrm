Introduction

The industrial internship was completed at MSSP.GL LLP from March 9, 2026 to May 2, 2026. During the internship, the main practical task was the design, implementation, and iterative improvement of a web-based CRM system named KZCRMS.

The relevance of the selected topic is determined by the fact that many service businesses still manage appointments, clients, and payments through messengers, spreadsheets, and disconnected tools. This creates scheduling conflicts, weak client retention, limited reporting capabilities, and a high dependence on manual coordination by administrators.

The internship therefore focused on the creation of a unified information system that combines client management, appointment scheduling, service configuration, staff workload tracking, payment handling, reporting, onboarding, and basic access control in a single SaaS-oriented product.

1. General Characteristics of the Internship Base

MSSP.GL LLP acted as the internship base and provided the environment for applied development, testing, and product iteration. The internship was organized around a real software engineering workflow: requirements clarification, interface revision, backend implementation, testing, deployment, documentation, and iterative improvement based on practical scenarios.

Within this environment, the internship tasks were directly connected with the creation of a commercial-grade CRM system suitable for service businesses such as dental clinics, barbershops, salons, studios, and other appointment-based organizations.

2. Purpose, Objectives, and Subject of the Internship

The purpose of the internship was to apply academic knowledge in software engineering to the development of a practical CRM web application and to improve the product up to a level suitable for future business use.

The main objectives of the internship were:

  • to design the overall architecture of the CRM system;
  • to implement the main business entities: clients, staff, services, appointments, payments, and business settings;
  • to improve usability, navigation, and product logic for real administrators;
  • to organize secure authentication, access control, and account verification;
  • to prepare the project for cloud deployment and domain-based operation;
  • to test, debug, and document the implemented solution.

Week 1. The first stage included familiarization with the internship goals, analysis of the future CRM concept, clarification of the main user roles, and preparation of the initial project structure.

Week 2. The application data model and first business entities were developed. The project began to support clients, staff members, services, appointments, and payments as core CRM components.

4. Development of the KZCRMS CRM System

KZCRMS was developed as a web-based CRM platform for service businesses where appointment scheduling and client retention are operationally critical. The application is built on Django and includes a public landing page, authentication pages, business dashboard, appointment journal, client cards, staff cards, financial reporting, configurable payment methods, profile management, settings, and trial-based access control.

The system supports multiple industry templates and adapts terminology for different niches while preserving a common product core. This makes it possible to use the same application for dentistry, barbershops, salons, studios, clinics, and similar businesses without duplicating the product architecture.

A significant amount of work was devoted to product quality: better empty states, more consistent wording, refined onboarding logic, a clearer distinction between profile and settings, a custom domain, working email verification, Google OAuth, and a more serious visual style for both the CRM interface and the landing page.

5. Security and Reliability Measures

A separate block of internship work was devoted to the security and reliability of the system. The goal was not only to implement business functionality, but also to reduce risks that typically appear in early-stage web products.

First, production configuration was hardened. The application was configured to reject startup in production when a real SECRET_KEY is not provided. At the same time, secure cookie flags, HTTPS redirection, HSTS, and browser-level protective headers were added.

Second, authentication-related abuse was limited by a basic rate-limiting mechanism for login, registration, and password reset requests. The limitation is IP-based and prevents rapid repeated attempts that could be used for brute-force attacks or abuse of public forms.

Third, destructive actions were made safer. Previously, some delete operations could be triggered through simple links. This was corrected by moving such actions to POST-only views and by using CSRF-protected forms in templates.

Fourth, the registration flow was revised so that internal email transport errors are no longer exposed directly to end users. Instead of leaking low-level SMTP or API details, the system now shows a safe user-facing message while logging the technical exception internally.

Finally, automated tests were added to verify that the implemented security measures behave correctly. These tests cover safe error handling, request method restrictions, and rate-limiting behavior.

6. Practical Results Obtained During the Internship

As a result of the internship, a working CRM prototype evolved into a significantly more complete SaaS-oriented product. The system now includes public entry points, protected business logic, configurable business entities, authentication options, trial access support, cloud deployment, custom domain operation, and a strengthened security baseline.

The project demonstrates both practical programming work and applied systems thinking: not only were individual functions implemented, but the application was shaped as a coherent product intended for future client use.

Conclusion

The industrial internship made it possible to apply theoretical software engineering knowledge to a real product-oriented project. During the internship, the KZCRMS system was designed and refined as a CRM platform for appointment-based service businesses.

The completed work covered backend logic, frontend structure, authentication, deployment, business workflows, security hardening, testing, and documentation. The result is a substantially improved system that can serve as the basis for further product growth and future commercial use.
